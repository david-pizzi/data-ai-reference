Content Governance

Endorsement, sharing, sensitivity labels, and content management in Fabric
Module 8 Reference
Sections: Endorsement Overview Promotion Certification Master Data Sensitivity Labels Item Sharing Job Scheduling Knowledge Check
Endorsement Overview
Promotion — any item owner or writer can promote content to signal it is valuable and ready for broader use within the organization
Certification — only authorized reviewers (designated by Fabric admin) can certify content, indicating it meets organizational quality standards
Master Data — the highest level of endorsement; marks core single-source-of-truth data such as customer lists, product codes, and reference tables
Progressive trust — endorsement levels build progressively — from promoted (team-level trust) to certified (org-level trust) to master data (canonical source)
Promotion
Who can promote — any item owner or user with write permissions on the item; no admin authorization required
Encourages collaborative use — promoting an item signals to colleagues that it is ready and useful, encouraging discovery and reuse
Available for all Fabric items — works across lakehouses, notebooks, pipelines, reports, semantic models, and more — except dashboards
Visual indicator — promoted items display a badge in the OneLake catalog and workspace, making them easy to find
Certification
Authorized by Fabric admin — only users or groups explicitly authorized by the Fabric administrator can certify content
Meets org quality standards — certification signals that the item has been reviewed and meets the organization’s established quality and governance criteria
Reliable and authoritative — certified items are considered the go-to source for org-wide reporting and analytics
Discoverable — certified items are prioritized in search results and the OneLake catalog, making them easy for users to find and trust
Master Data
Authorized reviewers only — like certification, only users designated by the Fabric admin can apply the master data endorsement
Core single-source-of-truth — identifies data that serves as the canonical, definitive source for key business entities (customer lists, product codes, geography tables)
Only for data items — master data endorsement applies specifically to data items such as lakehouses, warehouses, semantic models, and KQL databases — not to reports or notebooks
Prevents duplication — clearly marking master data helps teams avoid creating redundant copies and ensures everyone works from the same trusted source
Sensitivity Labels
Microsoft Information Protection — apply sensitivity labels from Microsoft Information Protection (MIP) directly to Fabric items to classify data by confidentiality level
Classify and protect data — labels like Confidential, Highly Confidential, or Public travel with the data — even when exported to Excel, PDF, or PowerPoint
Integration with Purview — sensitivity labels are managed through Microsoft Purview, providing a unified governance experience across Microsoft 365, Azure, and Fabric
Downstream inheritance — when a label is applied to a data source, it can automatically propagate to downstream items like reports and dashboards built on that data
Item Sharing
Share items across workspaces — share specific Fabric items (reports, semantic models, lakehouses) with users who do not have workspace access
Reshare permissions — when sharing, you can grant or deny the recipient’s ability to reshare the item with others
Workspace roles control sharing — Admin, Member, and Contributor roles can share items; Viewers cannot share unless explicitly granted reshare permission
Link-based sharing — generate shareable links for items with configurable access levels — people in your org, people with existing access, or specific people
Job Scheduling
Schedule recurring jobs — configure Fabric items such as pipelines, notebooks, and dataflows to run on a schedule (hourly, daily, weekly, or custom cron)
Delta Lake interoperability — scheduled jobs work seamlessly with Delta Lake tables, ensuring data remains fresh and consistent for downstream consumers
Table maintenance optimization — schedule table maintenance tasks like OPTIMIZE (bin-compaction) and VACUUM (old file cleanup) to keep Delta tables performing well
Monitoring and alerts — track job run history, view success/failure status, and configure alerts to be notified when scheduled jobs fail

What distinguishes certification from promotion?
Certification is available to all users, promotion requires admin approval
Certification requires authorization from the Fabric admin and signals org-wide quality standards
Certification applies only to reports, while promotion applies to all items
Which items can receive the master data endorsement?
All Fabric items including reports, notebooks, and dashboards
Only Power BI reports and dashboards
Data items such as lakehouses, warehouses, and semantic models
How do sensitivity labels travel with Fabric data?
They only apply within the Fabric workspace and are removed on export
Labels persist even when data is exported to Excel, PDF, or PowerPoint
They are applied only at the tenant level and cannot be set per item
