The Shift: Data Platform → Intelligence Platform
Before IQ: Fabric was a data platform — ingest, store, transform, query, report. Powerful, but fundamentally about moving and shaping data.
With IQ: Fabric becomes an intelligence platform. Instead of just storing and querying data, Fabric now understands business meaning through ontologies, reasons over relationships via graph, and acts through AI agents.
The progression: Data Lake → Lakehouse → Semantic Models → Ontology → Graph → Agents. Each step adds more meaning and more autonomy.
Key insight: IQ doesn't replace the existing Fabric workloads — it builds on top of them. OneLake stores the data, semantic models define measures, and IQ adds business context that enables AI agents to reason and act.
Core Concepts
ConceptWhat It IsWhy It Matters
Ontology Machine-readable model of business vocabulary. Defines entity types (Customer, Product, Order), their properties, and relationships — all bound to real data in OneLake. Semantic layer beyond BI. While semantic models define measures and aggregations, ontologies define what things are and how they relate. Foundation for AI agents to reason about business context.
Graph Native graph storage and compute. Nodes represent entity instances, edges represent relationships. Supports GQL for traversals and path finding. Ontology auto-generates a queryable graph. Enables questions like "which suppliers connect to our highest-risk products?" that are impractical with relational queries alone.
Data Agents Virtual analysts that use the ontology as grounding. Convert natural language questions into structured queries against the graph and underlying data. Grounded in ontology definitions, so answers are consistent and auditable. Not hallucinating — the agent reasons over defined business concepts and real data.
Operations Agents Autonomous agents that monitor real-time streams, detect constraint violations, and trigger workflows. Use ontology rules as an operational playbook. Connects to Azure AI Foundry for orchestration. Enables proactive, always-on business monitoring — not just dashboards you check, but agents that act when conditions are met.

Why This Matters: Creating Reusable IP
Early-mover advantage: Understanding Fabric IQ early gives a significant edge. Most practitioners are still focused on traditional data engineering and BI workloads.
Ontology design patterns: Position to create reusable templates for common industry ontologies — retail, healthcare, financial services, public sector.
Agent integration architectures: Document how data agents and operations agents connect to Azure AI Foundry, Copilot Studio, and existing enterprise workflows.
Migration guidance: Help customers evolve from traditional BI semantic models to full ontology-backed intelligence — a natural upsell path from existing Fabric deployments.
The strategic angle: IQ is where Microsoft is investing for the next wave. Being someone who can articulate the ontology-to-agent story is a differentiated position in the field.
